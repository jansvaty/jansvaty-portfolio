The Challenge

Why many potential investors never begin

Many potential investors never begin. Despite growing platforms like Sharesies, a significant portion of their target audience remains on the sidelines, held back by fear, confusion, and a lack of financial confidence. Our team was brought in to understand why, and design a solution to help.

Discovery

Focusing on the people Sharesies hadn't yet reached

We chose to focus exclusively on non-Sharesies users, people who had never signed up or who had dropped off, since Sharesies' existing research had only covered active customers. Our research combined desk research with in-depth interviews, followed by affinity mapping to surface patterns. From 32 findings we distilled 8 key insights.

"How might we better understand the attitudes and behaviours that non-Sharesies users have towards money and investing?"

Design Direction

A pivot driven by what the research actually said

Initial concepts explored integrating education into the sign-up flow, but testing quickly showed this created friction rather than reducing it. We pivoted. A competitive audit revealed a clear gap in Sharesies' educational offering. We tested two low-fidelity directions:

Structured desktop concept Interactive mobile concept

Concept testing with 4 participants revealed users wanted a combination of both, structured step-by-step content with interactive elements woven throughout.

The Solution

Investing Basics Mini Course

A structured, interactive learning experience embedded in the Sharesies platform, helping new investors grasp the basics in 5 steps, at their own pace.

Sharesies design system Custom illustrations Step-by-step structure Knowledge quiz Accessibility considered
Validation

Two rounds of testing, four key improvements

Usability testing with 4 participants across two rounds led to targeted improvements: search bar added, step directory hierarchy clarified, reading time placement fixed, and quiz stats expanded.

Reflection

What I took away

Learnings

The mid-project pivot was the most valuable moment. Letting research lead, even when it means abandoning early work, produces stronger outcomes.

Good team communication keeps a project on track when things shift.

Every design decision needs a clear why behind it.
