Sharesies

Investing 101

Sharesies is a stock market investing platform with a mission to make wealth development accessible to all. While investing offers a powerful path to financial empowerment, significant barriers still prevent many potential investors from getting started.

Year

2022

Client

Sharesies

Deliverables

Hi-fi prototype, final report

My role

UX/UI Design, Project coordinator

Challenge

Why many potential investors never begin.

Sharesies makes stock market investing more accessible, but the team wanted to better understand the barriers stopping new users from taking the first step. Our three-person team was asked to identify those barriers and explore how they differed across users.

The goal was to move from problem framing to a concept that could support first-time investors with clearer education, reduced friction, and stronger confidence.

Discovery

Desk research, interviews, and synthesis.

We began with desk research to understand how people across New Zealand and beyond approach investing. We then spoke directly with non-Sharesies users, including people who had considered joining and those who had signed up but stopped using the platform.

Research scope

We studied patterns in how people learn about and engage with investing, then interviewed users outside the Sharesies ecosystem to surface unfiltered perspectives on the barriers they faced.

What emerged

We mapped 32 findings into eight key insights. A strong pattern emerged: investing felt intimidating, overly complex, and culturally distant from many potential users.

Problem framing

We used the research to define a clear how-might-we direction around helping people overcome uncertainty and build enough confidence to start investing.

Design Direction

From workshops to an educational hub.

After a workshop with Sharesies, we narrowed our focus and tested a few concept directions. We briefly explored embedding learning into the sign-up flow, but research pointed us toward a better opportunity: improve the educational journey itself rather than adding more friction to onboarding.

We reviewed competitor learning sites and found a consistent pattern. Strong investing education combines step-by-step content, engaging visuals, interactive tools, and plain language explanations.

Solution

A mini course for investing basics.

Two low-fidelity concepts led to a high-fidelity concept built around structured learning, illustrations, quizzes, and practical written content. The final result was an Investing Basics Mini Course inside the Sharesies learning experience.

Learners moved through a guided path of short explanations and interactive elements, with clear language tied directly to the needs identified during research.

Validation

Testing concepts with real users.

We tested our concepts with inexperienced investors throughout the process. Early rounds used low-fidelity prototypes to validate the overall approach before committing to detail.

Testing confirmed the structured course format resonated strongly. Users appreciated the plain language and step-by-step structure, and felt more confident about investing after completing the content.

Reflection

What this project reinforced.

This project reinforced a few core lessons. Strong team communication matters more than any individual contribution, every design decision needs a clear reason grounded in research, and adaptability is essential when the problem space shifts partway through.

It also showed me that the most valuable insights often come from people who are not yet using a product. Their honest perspective is what shapes a better starting point.
